Portfolio

Some my favourite projects. I love to talk about them so feel free to ask me questions!

Wire Cutter

I needed to cut thousands of pieces of wire to precise lengths. Instead of spending hours cutting those wires by hand, I created an automated machine to do it!


Periodic Table

My girlfriend and I have an element collection. It's like collecting the building-blocks of the universe! Each one has unique properties and asthetics. Together, we build this LED display complete with a phone app to control various light-up configurations and animations.

Privacy Glasses

I have modified an LCD monitor to display a blank screen. To everyone else the monitor is broken. However, the wearer of these glasses can view the contents of the screen with ease.


Maze

I designed this maze as a starter project for students at Unleash Space to create on the CNC router and laser cutter. Tilt the maze to direct the little ball bearing to the center!


Tesla Coil

A Tesla Coil is an air-core transformer. My coil is capable of generating 500,000 volt meter-long lightning bolts.



Password Manager

Do you find it hard to remember strong unique passwords for different sites? This device remembers all your passwords and automaically types them by emulating a USB keyboard. Controlled by a phone app over bluetooth.

Capacitor Bank

This capacitor bank is made from brine-filled glass bottles wrapped in foil. It is capable of storing and releasing charge like a battery.

Chronograph

My chronograph uses a infrared dual light gate to measure the speed of a projectile traveling through the gates.

Digital Noticeboard

The digital noticeboard lives in my flat's kitchen and displays a daily joke, wallpaper and a weekly rotating job roster.

Ohm

I have co-founded an early start-up developing performance enhancing heat suits for cycling.


Little Robot

This little robot created by me and my girlfriend can navigate autonomously. This photo also came second in the 2014 University of Auckland Science photo competition.

Dodecahedron

I made this dodecahedron infinity mirror as an attention grabbing artifact for Shwoop (A digital engatement start-up I am part of).
